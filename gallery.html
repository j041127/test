Chairs

An upholstered chair made of ash, oak or birch, produced in lobby and lounge versions.

Moulded plywood and Isokon’s skill in producing it are the key technical factors that determined the chair.

The EVO-C began with a question from one of Vitra’s product developing engineers: could we achieve a one-piece moulded cantilever chair with two front legs, in other words could we go a step further than the Panton chair and design a two-legged version as a single plastic moulding?

A lounge and dining chair with accompanying table. The ash frames are stackable. The canvas slings come in a range of colours.

A leather-upholstered low chair.

A moulded-leather or fabric upholstered lounge chair with a satin stainless-steel base.

An extensive family of lights with hand-blown opal glass diffusers. There are models for the wall, the ceiling, the floor and for tables.

Wall and ceiling lights consisting of spun aluminium with a painted or anodised finish and a thermoplastic shade. Produced in two sizes.

A wall-mounted uplight, in four finishes.

A pendant light in powder-coated aluminium with a plastic diffuser.

A family of three sizes of paper lantern, sold in flat boxes. This was part of a project to revive the contemporary design of this Japanese tradition, originally conceived by Isamu Noguchi as Akari.
